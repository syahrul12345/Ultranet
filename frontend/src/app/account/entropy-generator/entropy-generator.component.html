The Ultra wallet uses a seed phrase, shown below, like most Bitcoin wallets. Your public and private keys are entirely derived from this seed phrase, and then these keys are used to secure your account and sign transactions on the network. For this reason, you will always be able to recover your account as long as you have the seed phrase. Also for this reason, losing your seed phrase could render your account inaccessible if, for example, you install this software on another machine. Just to reiterate:
You could lose access to your account and any Ultra you've purchased if you lose your seed phrase.
The public and private keys generated from this seed phrase are effectively used as your identity on the network. Any message you send will be end-to-end encrypted using these keys. Any transaction you initiate will be signed by these keys. So it's pretty important you not lose your seed phrase.
Write down your seed phrase from below and keep it in a safe place.

Because there is a standard for generating seed phrases for Bitcoin (called BIP39), and because we use this same standard to generate Ultranet seed phrases, Ultranet seed phrases can also be used with any Bitcoin wallet that implements this standard under the m/44'/0'/0'/0/0 derivation path (e.g. Mycelium, Electrum). Similarly, any standard Bitcoin seed phrase you have can also be used as an Ultranet seed phrase. Of course, all of this can be verified using a tool like the Ian Coleman mnemonic generator (must click "show entropy details") so you know this generator is doing what it says it is.
